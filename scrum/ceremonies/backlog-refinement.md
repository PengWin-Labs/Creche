# Backlog Refinement
> Ceremony · Duration: ~30 min · Week 2 Day 1 + Week 3 Day 1

---

## Purpose

Keep the top of the backlog sprint-ready. Refinement is maintenance work — without it, sprint planning becomes slow and messy.

---

## When

- **Refinement #1:** Midpoint of sprint (Day 8–10). Focus on next sprint's candidates.
- **Refinement #2:** ~1 week before sprint end (Day 15–17). Final readiness check.

---

## Runbook

### Step 1: Triage new inbound (~10 min)

Review anything that's landed in the backlog since last refinement:
- Is it actually worth building? If not, archive it now.
- Does it belong to an existing epic, or is it standalone?
- Rough size: small (1–2 pts), medium (3–5 pts), large (8+ pts)?

Large items: note that they need breaking down before they're sprint-ready.

---

### Step 2: Refine the top 10 (~15 min)

For each of the top 10 backlog items:

- [ ] Is the title clear and actionable?
- [ ] Is there an acceptance criteria section? ("Done when...")
- [ ] Is it sized (story points assigned)?
- [ ] Are dependencies noted?
- [ ] Is it actually still relevant / prioritized correctly?

If any item fails 2+ of these checks, fix it now or drop it lower in the backlog.

---

### Step 3: Reprioritize if needed (~5 min)

- Did anything change this sprint that shifts priorities?
- Is the sprint goal for next sprint starting to take shape? Promote relevant items.
- Archive anything that's been in the top 20 for 2+ sprints without being selected.

---

## Output

- Top 10 backlog items are refinement-ready
- Oversized items have break-down notes or sub-tasks
- Anything stale is archived

---

## Signs Refinement Is Working

- Sprint planning takes <1 hr
- Issues enter sprints with clear acceptance criteria already written
- You rarely have to write criteria mid-sprint

## Signs It's Not

- Sprint planning regularly runs long because issues aren't clear
- You pull issues into sprints that turn out to be bigger than expected
- The backlog has 50+ items and nothing is prioritized
