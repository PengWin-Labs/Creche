# Sprint Retrospective
> Ceremony · Duration: ~30 min · Last day of sprint, after Review

---

## Purpose

Improve the process. Not what was built — how you worked. The retro is where the compounding happens: each sprint should be slightly more effective than the last because you learned something and changed something.

The rule: **every retro must produce at least one concrete action**. Observations without actions are just venting.

---

## Runbook

### 1. Set the lens (~2 min)

Pick a framing question to keep the retro focused. Options:
- What slowed me down most this sprint?
- What would I do differently if I ran this sprint again?
- What's the biggest process risk heading into next sprint?
- What did I do well that I should protect?

---

### 2. What went well? (~8 min)

List 3–5 things that worked. Be specific — not "I was productive" but "breaking that auth task into 3 sub-tasks meant I could ship it incrementally without blocking myself."

Note what you want to explicitly repeat next sprint.

---

### 3. What didn't go well? (~10 min)

List friction points, slippages, surprises. For each:
- What happened?
- Was it a one-time thing or a pattern?
- Is it within your control to change?

Don't spend more than 1–2 minutes per item — identify, don't dwell.

---

### 4. Experiments for next sprint (~10 min)

From the friction above, pick **1–2 process changes** to try next sprint. Frame them as experiments, not commitments:

> "Next sprint I'll try [X] to see if it reduces [friction]."

Examples:
- "I'll block 9–11am as no-meeting/no-Slack deep work to reduce context switching."
- "I'll write acceptance criteria for every issue before adding it to planning."
- "I'll do standup at end of day instead of start to log what actually happened."

Add these as explicit sprint notes in the planning doc for next sprint.

---

### 5. Metrics update (~5 min)

Record in `journal/metrics.md`:

| Sprint | Velocity | Goal met? | Carryover % | Experiment result |
|--------|----------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| [N]    | [pts]    | Y/P/N     | [%]         | [if applicable]   |

---

## Output

- What went well (noted for repeat)
- 1–2 friction points identified
- 1–2 experiments committed for next sprint
- Metrics row updated
