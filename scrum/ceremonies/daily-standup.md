# Daily Standup
> Ceremony · Duration: ~10 min · Every weekday

---

## Purpose

Not a status report — a daily calibration. Are you on track to hit the sprint goal? If not, what's in the way?

---

## Format

Solo standups are async by default. Write it, don't say it. This creates a log and forces slightly more honest self-assessment than mental note-taking.

Write in your daily journal (`journal/YYYY-MM-DD.md`) or directly in Linear as a sprint comment.

---

## Three Questions

**1. What did I complete yesterday?**
List issues closed or meaningfully advanced. Be specific — vague entries ("worked on auth") are a signal that the day was unfocused.

**2. What am I doing today?**
Name 1–3 issues. Not a full task list — the most important thing(s) that move the sprint goal forward.

**3. What's in my way?**
Blockers, decisions needed, unknowns that need resolving. If blank for multiple days in a row, either the sprint is easy (good) or you're not surfacing real friction (bad).

---

## Rules

- **Time-box strictly.** 10 minutes. If a blocker needs deeper thought, schedule a separate session for it — don't let it expand standup.
- **Don't skip it.** On days you don't feel like doing it, those are often the days most worth doing it. A blank standup log is a signal to investigate.
- **Fridays:** Add a brief week-in-review line — was this a good week of sprint progress? Red / yellow / green.

---

## Signs Standup Is Working

- Your "today" list is consistently 1–3 items (not 10)
- Blockers surface within 24 hrs of appearing
- You can tell, mid-sprint, whether you're on track for the goal without checking Linear

## Signs It's Not

- Entries are vague or copy-pasted
- The blockers section is always blank
- "Today" keeps repeating the same issue multiple days running
