# Sprint Review
> Ceremony · Duration: ~30 min · Last day of sprint (Day 28)

---

## Purpose

Demo what was built. Evaluate against the sprint goal. Decide what this means for the product and roadmap. At N=1, you play both builder and stakeholder — the discipline is to actually step back and evaluate honestly, not just rubber-stamp your own work.

---

## Runbook

### 1. Goal check (~5 min)

State the sprint goal (written at planning). Ask:

- Was the goal met? Yes / Partially / No
- If partially or no: what prevented it? (Honest answer — note for retro)

---

### 2. Walk the completed work (~15 min)

For each closed issue, briefly:
- What did it do?
- Does it work as intended? (Actually test it — don't assume)
- Any known limitations or follow-up needed?

For any incomplete or carried-over issues:
- Why didn't it finish?
- Still relevant? Re-enter or archive?

---

### 3. Stakeholder perspective (~5 min)

Put on the "user / future stakeholder" hat:
- If someone used the product today, what would they experience that's new?
- Does the product feel meaningfully closer to the vision than 4 weeks ago?
- What's the most important thing it still can't do?

---

### 4. Roadmap update (~5 min)

- Does anything need to move based on what you learned this sprint?
- Any new epics or themes that emerged?
- Update the roadmap / backlog in Linear to reflect current thinking.

---

## Output

- Sprint goal outcome recorded (met / partial / missed + reason)
- Completed issues reviewed and confirmed
- Carryover items re-evaluated
- Roadmap adjusted if needed
