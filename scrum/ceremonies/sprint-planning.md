# Sprint Planning
> Ceremony · Duration: ~1 hr · Day 1 of each sprint

---

## Purpose

Set the sprint goal. Select and commit to a slice of backlog that serves that goal. Walk out with a clear plan for the next 4 weeks.

---

## Before You Start

- [ ] Backlog top 10 are refined (sized, acceptance criteria written)
- [ ] Previous sprint is closed out in Linear (all done items marked, carryover re-evaluated)
- [ ] You know your approximate available capacity this sprint (planned leave, travel, etc.)

---

## Runbook

### 1. Put on the Product Owner hat (~15 min)

Review what matters most right now:

- What's the single most valuable thing the product or business needs in the next 4 weeks?
- What would make this sprint feel like a success to a future user or stakeholder?
- What's the highest-risk unknown that needs resolving?

**Write the sprint goal (one sentence):**
> By end of sprint, ___[who]___ can ___[do/see/experience]___ ___[outcome]___, which enables ___[value]___.

Commit to this goal. It doesn't change mid-sprint without a good reason.

---

### 2. Put on the Scrum Master hat (~15 min)

Translate the goal into a sprint scope:

- What issues in the backlog directly serve this goal? Pull candidates.
- Estimate total points. Compare to your recent velocity (or use 20–30 pts as a first-sprint default).
- Leave ~20% buffer for unplanned work and bugs. Don't fill every hour.
- Confirm: can this scope realistically deliver the sprint goal?

---

### 3. Put on the Developer hat (~15 min)

Review each committed issue:

- Is the acceptance criteria clear enough to start building?
- Are there any dependencies or blockers you can see now?
- Break down any 8-point issues into smaller tasks.
- Sequence the first 3–5 issues to tackle in Week 1.

---

### 4. Finalize & Document (~15 min)

- Set sprint goal in Linear (Cycle description field)
- All committed issues moved into the active Cycle
- Add sprint planning note to `journal/` with:
  - Sprint goal
  - Committed point total
  - Top 3 anticipated risks
  - One "if I have extra capacity" issue (stretch goal)

---

## Output

- Sprint goal written and visible in Linear
- Active cycle populated
- Sprint planning journal entry created
